The Problem

Filling FLSSI forms is a tax on every probate matter.

The official 2025 FLSSI forms (delivered through Clio Draft) work, but they make you pay for every petition, oath, and order you file.

  • Zero data sharing between forms. Type the decedent's SSN on the Petition, type it again on the Oath, again on the Letters, again on the Notice to Creditors. Nothing talks to anything.
  • One FLSSI form per variant. Testate vs intestate, single vs multiple petitioners, domiciliary vs ancillary — each combination is its own form ID to hunt down. Twelve different Petition for Administration variants alone.
  • Formatting breaks under real data. FLSSI's two-column layout looks fine empty. Drop a multi-line address into a justified paragraph and the text spreads across the page with six-word-wide gaps.
What I Built

One app, browser-based, built around how our firm actually files.

Client-side only — documents generate in the browser and never leave my machine. Data lives in Supabase with row-level security.

✺

Smart conditional templates

ONE Petition for Administration template covers testate/intestate × single/multiple petitioners × domiciliary/ancillary. No more hunting through 12 different FLSSI form IDs. The document branches itself based on the matter.

▸

Open Estate wizard

Five questions — Formal/Summary, Testate/Intestate, Domiciliary/Ancillary, Single/Multiple petitioners, County — and the right filing bundle loads itself. Broward locals auto-attach when the county is Broward.

↻

Cross-form data sharing

Enter the decedent's SSN once. It populates every form in that matter — petition, oath, letters, notice, inventory. The PR's address. Bar numbers. Dates of death. Type it once, it's everywhere.

§

Per-matter signing attorney

Switch between David or Jill at the matter level. The signature block, bar number, firm email, and phone all swap automatically. Guardianship defaults to Jill; probate defaults to David; override either way when it matters.

⚖

County-specific AI certifications

Broward's AO 2026-03-Gen and Miami-Dade's AO 26-04 generative-AI certifications are opt-in per form via a "Generative AI Disclosure" checkbox (default off). When the user flips it on and the matter's county matches, the correct AO language renders verbatim above the signature block. Judge-signed orders and letters never carry the cert — by design.

$

Typed asset rows with totals

Estate assets are a repeating group with a real numeric field for value. The template renders $485,000.00, and totals compute automatically — available for the petition, the inventory, and anything else I wire into the same matter.

№

Native Word numbering

Paragraphs use Word's actual numbering engine — not a hardcoded "1." in front of the text. Which means when you or a paralegal edits the generated .docx and inserts a paragraph, the rest of the document renumbers itself. The way a legal document should.

⊞

Local rules bundled in

Broward and Miami-Dade mandatory checklists, criminal history affidavits, and local-form requirements are part of the filing bundle. Pull Open Estate (intestate, Broward) and you get the Petition, Order, Oath, Letters, Designation of Email, Broward opening checklist, and criminal history affidavit — together.

Under the Hood

Deliberately boring stack.

Nothing fancy. No framework lock-in. Works on any modern browser and will still work in five years.

Frontend
Vanilla JavaScript. One app.js, one index.html, one styles.css. No React, no build step.
Docs
docxtemplater + PizZip, in-browser. .docx files generate client-side — no server ever sees the data.
Storage
Supabase Postgres with row-level security. Admin sees all rows; standard users see their own.
Auth
Microsoft 365 OAuth. Sign in with your firm email; auto-admin on the allow-list.
Templates
Python builder scripts emit .docx templates deterministically — no hand-editing Word files.
Hosting
Static site on GitHub Pages. Deploys on git push. No backend to babysit.
