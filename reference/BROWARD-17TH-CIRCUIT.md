## Documents Required to Open a New File

### 1. Formal Administration (Chapter 733, Florida Statutes)

#### TESTATE (WITH A WILL)
- Petition to Admit Will (P3-0100)
- Order Admitting Will and Appointing Personal Representative (P3-0420)
- Oath of Personal Representative and Designation of Resident Agent (P3-0600)
- Letters of Administration (P3-0700)
- Death Certificate (original — Broward requires original, not scan)
- Original will deposited with Broward County Clerk of Court
- Affidavit Regarding Criminal History (BW-0010) — local form, required for ALL filings
- **Mandatory Checklist** (BW-0020) — Clerk will NOT forward petition without it

#### INTESTATE (WITHOUT A WILL)
- Petition to Appoint Personal Representative (P3-0120)
- Order Appointing Personal Representative (P3-0440)
- Oath of Personal Representative and Designation of Resident Agent (P3-0600)
- Letters of Administration (P3-0700)
- Any necessary consents to the appointment of the Personal Representative
- Death Certificate (original)
- Affidavit Regarding Criminal History (BW-0010)
- Affidavit of Heirs — local form, required for ALL intestate cases
- **Mandatory Checklist** — Clerk will NOT forward petition without it

#### BONDS (Broward-specific policy)
All non-residents of Florida must post a bond prior to appointment as PR. Bond = value of estate assets. Can be posted at initial filing or wait until the Court enters the Order. Per Florida Statutes, the decision to require a bond is within Court discretion even if waived in the will or PR is sole beneficiary.

### 2. Summary Administration (Sections 735.201-735.202)

Qualifies if: (1) probate assets < $75,000 (excluding exempt property) OR (2) decedent dead > 2 years.

- Petition for Summary Administration
- Original Will (if applicable)
- Order Admitting Will (if applicable)
- Proof of Paid Funeral Expenses and Last Medical Expenses
- Death Certificate
- Order of Summary Administration
- **Mandatory Checklist**

Small estates (eff. 1/1/2000): Dept. of Revenue no longer requires tax return filing; PR may execute affidavit attesting non-taxable. Court may consider affidavit per section 198.32(2) as evidence of non-liability.

### 3. Disposition of Personal Property Without Administration (Section 735.301)

Decedent must be Broward County resident or have maintained property in Broward. Allows reimbursement for reasonable funeral/medical expenses within 60 days of death. Up to $1,000 of exempt personal property under section 732.402.

### 4. Guardianships (Chapter 744)

- Petition for Appointment of Guardian
- Letters of Guardianship
- Order Appointing Guardian
- Mandatory Checklist (must use Broward County Smart Form)
- Investigatory Cost ($50 to Clerk, if applicable)
- FDLE Cost (~$54.25 to FDLE, if applicable)

Note: Petition to Determine Incapacity (s. 744.3201) must also be filed with Petition for Appointment.

## Documents Required to Close an Estate (Section X)

- Death Certificate
- Inventory
- Proof of Publication of Notice to Creditors (NOT Notice of Administration — that's not sufficient)
- Florida Tax Release or Affidavit of No Florida Estate Tax Due (if Final Certificate needed, need Estate Tax Closing Letter)
- Proof that Agency for Health Care Administration (individuals over 55, per s. 733.2121(3)(d)) was served with notice to creditors, unless agency already filed a statement of claim and claim has been satisfied/disposed
- Receipt of Beneficiaries
- Verified Statement Regarding Creditors
- Resolution or Satisfaction of any/all claims filed against the Estate
- Final Accounting or Waivers of same by all Beneficiaries
- Petition for Discharge
- Schedule of Distribution (unless waived)
- Report of Distribution (unless waived)
- Order of Discharge

## Affidavit of Heirs — Structure (4 pages)

Required for ALL intestate cases (formal and summary). Must list ALL relatives of decedent.

1. Undersigned's relationship to estate (interest / no interest, related / not related, years known)
2. a. Spouse of decedent (name, age, address; if deceased: name and date of death)
   b. Former spouse(s) (name, age, address; if deceased/divorced: details)
3. a. Children of decedent, or descendants of deceased children (name, age, address; if deceased: name, date of death, grandchildren from predeceased children)
   b. Children not biologically related to both decedent and spouse (child's name + biological parent)
4. Grandchildren of decedent (name, age, address; if deceased: name and date of death)
5. Parents of decedent (name, age, address; if deceased: name and date of death)
6. Brothers and sisters, or descendants of deceased siblings (specify half-relatives; name, age, address; if deceased: name, date of death, plus children of predeceased siblings)
7. Nephews and nieces (name, age, address; if deceased: name and date of death)
8. Grandparents of decedent (name, age, address; if deceased: name and date of death)

Instructions: If decedent never had a relative in a category, write "None." If unknown, write "Unknown." Indicate half-relatives where applicable.

## Checklist for Formal Admin Testate — Key Items

(Full PDF saved in this folder)

**Hearing:** Choose ex-parte/motion/special set calendar OR submitted without hearing.

**Required verifications:**
- Death certificate filed
- Affidavit Regarding Criminal History filed
- Broward County residence on death cert (or property situs in petition if non-FL)
- Petition verified, signed by Petitioner AND attorney
- Petitioner's interest and address in Petition
- Will/codicil e-filed and original deposited with Clerk (OR Petition to Establish Lost/Destroyed Will filed)
- Will is self-proven (or oath of witness — NOTE: notary stamp is insufficient)
- Petitioner not a convicted felon, is FL resident (or related if non-FL)
- Correct beneficiaries listed with minor birthdates
- Assets and approximate values listed
- PR has preference of appointment (renunciations/death certs filed if needed)
- Oath of PR and resident agent designation filed
- Proposed order filed (4+ lines of text + case number on signature page)
- Proposed letters filed (4+ lines of text + case number on signature page)
- Trust beneficiary disclosure (if trust is a beneficiary)

**Certification A** (all items checked): Attorney certifies all required info filed; petition goes directly to Judge.
**Certification B** (items missing): List what's missing; petition reviewed by court staff first.

## Checklist for Discharge — Key Items

(Full PDF saved in this folder)

- Inventory filed
- Proof of publication of notice to creditors filed (OR decedent dead > 2 years)
- Affidavit of no estate tax due filed (OR decedent dead > 2 years)
- Statement regarding creditors filed (OR decedent dead > 2 years)
- Notice to creditors served on Agency for Health Care Administration (OR decedent < 55 OR dead > 2 years)
- No claims filed (OR all claims paid/settled with satisfaction filed)
- Petition for Discharge signed by PR
- Residuary beneficiaries consented and waived accounting (OR served and objection period expired)
- Petition served on unsatisfied creditors (OR none exist)
- Proposed order: 4+ lines of text + case number on signature page
- Trust beneficiary disclosure (if applicable)

## Other Key Rules

### Electronic Submission of Orders (Section III)
- Orders without hearing can be submitted electronically via CMS
- Applicable: formal/summary admin petitions, homestead, sell real property, discharge, etc.
- Proposed order uploaded as PDF through CMS portal
- Must include certification clause

### Homestead (Section IX)
- **No stand-alone homestead petitions** — Broward judges will not entertain them
- Summary Admin: homestead petition filed within summary admin; no Notice to Creditors required before Homestead Order
- Formal Admin: Court will not enter Homestead Order until creditor period expires (3 months from first publication). Prior publication not necessary if decedent dead > 2 years.

### Reopening Estates (Section VIII.C)
- $50 filing fee
- Closed by Order of Discharge: file Petition + Order for Subsequent Administration
- If letters needed: file Petition to Reopen Estate for Further Administration (include language to revoke prior discharge orders)
- Newly discovered assets in summary admin: file Amended Petition + proposed Order

### Emergency Hearings (Section IV)
- Contact assigned judge's judicial assistant
- Must file emergency motion with detailed basis for emergency
- Opposing counsel must be notified before hearing

### Remote Appearances (Section VI)
- Available for most proceedings
- Contact judicial assistant for specific judge requirements
